Why this matters

Wrong filter = wasted money + wasted water

Most Indians use the wrong purifier for their water. It's not just harmful — it wastes thousands of litres of water every year.

⚠

RO on low TDS water strips minerals

If your TDS is below 150 ppm and you use RO, the output drops to 10–30 ppm — well below the safe mineral threshold. This water can leach calcium and magnesium from your bones over time.

💧

RO wastes 3–5 litres per litre purified

A standard RO purifier wastes 3–5 litres of water for every 1 litre it produces. On TDS below 300 ppm, this waste is completely unnecessary — UV+UF achieves the same result with zero waste.

🦠

UV/UF alone can't handle high TDS

Above 500 ppm, water contains dissolved salts, heavy metals, fluoride, and nitrates that UV and UF cannot remove. Only RO handles dissolved solids. Using just UV here gives false security.

🏥

High TDS causes long-term health damage

Consistently drinking water above 900 ppm is linked to kidney stones, hypertension, and gastrointestinal disorders. The dissolved solids accumulate over years, not days.

🗺

Water quality varies wildly across India

Delhi borewell water averages 600–900 ppm. Mumbai municipal averages 100–200 ppm. Rural Rajasthan can exceed 1500 ppm. One-size-fits-all purifier advice is simply wrong.
